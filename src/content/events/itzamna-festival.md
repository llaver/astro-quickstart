# ☀️ 🌖🔹ITZAMNA PSYTRANCE FESTIVAL🔹🌖☀️

In various myths, Itzamna is depicted as an old priest sitting on a throne wearing a tall cylindrical headdress that resembles a flower. He is commonly depicted as half caiman and half human, and is sometimes referred to as a two-headed underwater serpent. Another significant manifestation of Itzamna is the Bird of Heaven, which is also known as Itzam Yeh.

Itzamna is credited with bringing scientific and technological innovations to the Mayans by combining the elements of the earth, the underworld, and the heavens. He is regarded as a creator who made systems and processes that can only be described as scientific or logical. According to legend, Itzamna taught the Maya how to create various types of calendars which would be used to determine the ideal days for various activities such as farming and religious worship.

Itzamna was known to have provided the Maya with the foundations of civilization by reportedly teaching them how to grow corn, how to practice medicine, and how to follow the celestial movements of the sky.
